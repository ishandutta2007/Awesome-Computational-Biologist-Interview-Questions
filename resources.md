# Additional Resources

A short list of resources for going deeper on computational biology. (Not exhaustive — PRs welcome.) This field spans algorithms, statistics, and biology, and the tooling/methods evolve quickly — cross-check anything specific against current literature before an interview.

## Foundational Reading
- *Bioinformatics Algorithms: An Active Learning Approach* — Compeau & Pevzner. A strong, problem-driven introduction to the core algorithmic foundations of the field.
- *An Introduction to Systems Biology: Design Principles of Biological Circuits* — Uri Alon. An accessible, influential introduction to network motifs and dynamical thinking about biological circuits.
- *Statistical Methods in Bioinformatics* — Ewens & Grant. Solid statistical grounding specifically framed around biological data.
- Durbin, Eddy, Krogh & Mitchison's *Biological Sequence Analysis* — the classic, thorough reference on HMMs and probabilistic approaches to sequence analysis.

## Key Tools & Resources (good to be conversationally familiar with)
- BLAST and its variants — the standard reference point for sequence search/alignment heuristics.
- A structure prediction tool (e.g., AlphaFold and related methods) and its associated confidence metrics and documentation.
- A molecular dynamics simulation package (e.g., GROMACS or similar) if structural/biophysical modeling is relevant to your target role.
- A phylogenetics software package (e.g., RAxML, MrBayes, or similar) for maximum likelihood/Bayesian tree inference.
- Standard workflow management tools (Snakemake, Nextflow) for reproducible pipeline design.

## Communities & Discussion
- Biostars and the Bioinformatics Stack Exchange for practical, tooling-level Q&A.
- r/bioinformatics and r/compbio for broader discussion threads.
- ISCB (International Society for Computational Biology) resources and conference proceedings (e.g., ISMB) for a sense of current research directions.

## Staying Current
- This field moves quickly, particularly in structure prediction, systems modeling, and statistical methods for high-dimensional biological data — follow recent publications and preprints (bioRxiv, arXiv's q-bio category) from active research groups in your area of focus.
- Companion repos in this collection — **Genomics Data Scientist** and **AI Drug Discovery Scientist** — cover adjacent, partially overlapping territory (NGS pipelines/statistics, and pharma/cheminformatics applications respectively) that may also be relevant depending on your target role.
